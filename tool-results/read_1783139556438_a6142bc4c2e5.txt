   171→ Executive Summary
   172→ RZR Global — the AI-driven advertising platform formerly known as Aarki — is scaling across
   173→ eight global offices (San Francisco, New York, London, Tel Aviv, Bangalore, Beijing, Manila, and
   174→ Seoul), processing over 5 million mobile ad requests per second from 10 billion+ devices. As the
   175→ business expands, two structural gaps have surfaced that are directly impacting performance,
   176→ manager effectiveness, and cultural cohesion: the absence of a structured onboarding
   177→ experience and the lack of any formal manager or leadership capability development. This plan is
   178→ the operating blueprint for the Senior L&D; Lead role, designed to close those gaps and build the
   179→ learning infrastructure that defines how RZR develops its people through Year 1 and into Year 2.
   180→
   181→ The mandate is sequenced around two urgent priorities. First, within 30 days, revamp, rebuild, and
   182→ relaunch RZR Academy as a globally consistent onboarding experience across all markets. Second, within
   183→ 90 days, design and launch a structured Manager Capability Development program for first-time and new
   184→ managers globally. From there, the role expands into a full-stack L&D; calendar, leadership development,
   185→ function-specific training, and a measurement framework built on the Kirkpatrick L1–L3 model. The plan is
   186→ explicitly AI-native: every workflow considers AI augmentation for content generation, personalization,
   187→ translation, simulation, and analytics.

   373→ Strategic Approach & Guiding Principles
   374→ The L&D; function at RZR will be built on five operating principles. These are not slogans — they translate
   375→ into concrete design decisions across every workstream, from how content is authored to how programs
   376→ are measured and how the team is structured. The principles are deliberately aligned with RZR's own
   377→ cultural narrative of intelligence making impact and with the builder mindset the role demands.
   378→
   379→
   380→ The Five Principles
   381→
   382→                AI-Native by Default
   383→                Every workflow starts by asking: how does AI augment this? Content drafting, personalization,
   384→
   385→   P1           translation, simulation, analytics — AI is the operating layer, not a feature.
   386→
   387→                BEHAVIOR → LLM-assisted content (3–5x throughput), AI persona role-play for manager training,
   388→                AI-surfaced cohort insights.
   389→

   395→
   396→
   397→
   398→
   399→                Builder Mindset
   400→
   401→  P2
   402→                Design from zero. No legacy constraints, no inherited templates. Every artifact is built to be measured,
   403→                iterated, and either scaled or retired within 2 cycles.
   404→
   405→                BEHAVIOR → MVP-first launches; 90-day assessment on every cohort; explicit retire/iterate decisions.
   406→
   407→
   408→
   409→
   410→                Measurement-Obsessed
   411→                No program ships without a Kirkpatrick L1–L3 plan. Decisions to continue, improve, or retire are made
   412→
   413→   P3           on data, not opinion. Quarterly readout to ELT is non-negotiable.
   414→
   415→                BEHAVIOR → MEI v1 in 90 days; L1 surveys on every session; L2 knowledge checks; L3 30/60/90
   416→                follow-ups.
   417→
   418→
   419→
   420→
   421→                Globally Consistent, Locally Adapted
   422→
   423→  P4
   424→                One program architecture across all markets. Regional variants only where cultural, legal, or language
   425→                considerations demand. Avoid the trap of 8 separate onboarding programs.
   426→
   427→                BEHAVIOR → Single Academy architecture with regional overlay; local-language tracks by cohort.
   428→
   429→
   430→
   431→
   432→                Manager as Multiplier
   433→
   434→   P5
   435→                Manager capability is the highest-leverage L&D; investment. A great manager improves the
   436→                performance of 6–10 ICs; a poor one destroys it. Manager dev gets disproportionate resourcing.
   437→
   438→                BEHAVIOR → FTM program, Managers Learning Series, MEI driving manager promotion decisions.
   439→
   440→
   441→
   442→ These principles also serve as the design review criteria for every program. Before any initiative moves
   443→ from design to launch, it must pass a five-question check: Is AI used where it could augment? Is the
   444→ measurement plan defined? Is the global architecture preserved with regional variants only where
   445→ necessary? Does this build manager capability? Is there a 90-day effectiveness assessment built in?
   446→ Programs that cannot answer yes to all five are sent back to design.
